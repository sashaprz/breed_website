Cycle Life

If the electrolyte does not form a stable SEI, it will react with the anode/cathode and consume active lithium, causing capacity fade. Electrolyte degradation products can also accumulate on the anode/cathode surface, increasing internal impedance and accelerating capacity fade.

Energy Density

Energy is determined by Voltage * capacity / mass. Using anode and cathode materials with a higher electrochemical potential difference could increase battery voltage. However, current electrolytes become unstable and degrade at higher voltages, limiting the maximum voltage and thus the battery's energy density.

Safety

Current electrolytes are flammable, so battery overheating can trigger a self-sustaining fire (thermal runaway). Electrolyte degradation produces flammable, toxic gases that increase the internal pressure of the cell, increasing the risk of explosion.
